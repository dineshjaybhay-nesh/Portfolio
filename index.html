Porter Mini Truck Booking UX Evaluation & Redesign

Friction Diagnostic & Scope

Porter coordinates a diverse fleet of mini trucks and movers across 20+ cities. This teardown tackles the high drop-off zone of the user journey—**Zone 2: Booking Confirmation**. This is the highest leverage gate for improving checkout-to-booking conversions without re-architecting engineering frameworks.

Teardown Observations:

  • Technical Vehicle Codes: Naming schemas ("Mini 3W", "Tata Ace") fail to describe capacity or volume, forcing mental translation for non-freight shippers.
  • Recall Demands: Shippers must mentally cross-compare payload ceilings, parameters, and dynamic estimates while scrolling.
  • Buried Fare Summaries: GST validations and coupon triggers stack directly above payment breakdowns, pushing final payable fares below the viewport fold.

Heuristic Violations (Nielsen's 10 Principles)

Heuristic Friction Element Suggested Redesign
System & Real World Match Names match fleet operations, not residential realities. Integrate plain-language utility tags ("Fits Sofa + Fridge").
Recognition Over Recall Comparing weight limitations and dynamic quotes manually. Include persistent dimensional specifications directly on selector cards.
Visibility of Status Review screen displays no progress timeline or sequence steps. Add top-anchored layout navigation (Pickup → Vehicle → Confirm).
Error Recovery Location failure outputs raw "Retry" CTA without diagnostics. Identify point-of-failure (Wi-Fi, GPS settings) and direct setting links.

Cognitive & Spatial Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) Laws

Hick's Law

Choice overhead delays task completion. Listing 5+ non-grouped options slows vehicle matching.
Solution: Bucket fleet choices ("1 BHK Moves", "Box Delivery") and flag the top "Recommended" item.

Fitts's Law

Frequently adjusted modifiers ("Edit Address") sit far from comfortable thumb sweeps.
Solution: Bring key controls lower down the screen, optimizing mobile touch interfaces.

Jakob's Law

Users project booking models from high-frequency transport platforms like Uber/Ola.
Solution: Mimic intuitive linear checkpoints to ease drop-off anxiety.

Strategic Redesign Blueprints

Redesigned Screen Optimizations
  • Vehicle Selection: Added direct payload illustrations, capacity guides, and clear recommended context badges.
  • Booking Review: Elevated Fare Summary above secondary options; progress indicator pinned dynamically to header.
  • Location Error: Introduced smart diagnoses for GPS or network-related failures.
