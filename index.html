Porter Logistics UX Evaluation & Redesign Case Study

An exhaustive end-to-end audit of Porter's Mini Truck booking flow. Using Nielsen's 10 Usability Heuristics, Hick's Law, and Fitts's Law to diagnose friction and design booking-conversion upgrades.

Open Prototype in Tab

1. Objective & Walkthrough Scope

Porter is an on-demand logistics pioneer across 20+ Indian cities. This diagnostic case study targets **Zone 2: Booking Confirmation** (specifically vehicle fleet selection and detailed trip review). These steps hold the highest-leverage potential to prevent booking abandonment by first-time users.

Walkthrough Friction Points:

  • **Vehicle names are too technical:** casual shippers don't understand commercial freight models ("Mini 3W" or "Tata Ace") forcing mental estimation of volumes.
  • **Forced Memory / High Cognitive Load:** Users must memorize and cross-compare payload capacities and prices while scrolling long lists.
  • **Hidden Pricing Details:** GST and discount panels sit above the Fare Summary, pushing crucial payment numbers below the viewport fold.

2. Nielsen's Heuristic Violation Breakdown

Heuristic Violated Direct Observation Redesign Solutions
System & Real World Match Vehicle options labelled by technical engineering codes ("Tata Ace"). Integrate real-world cargo taglines (e.g., "Fits Sofa + Fridge", "Best for 1 BHK").
Recognition Over Recall Comparing weights & pricing metrics requires vertical backtracking. Render direct dimensional and load indicators persistently on each card.
Visibility of Status Booking review has zero layout tracking of current pipeline phase. Attach top-anchored layout tracking bar (Pickup → Vehicle → Confirm).
Error Diagnosis Location error provides an unhelpful blank state + "Retry" CTA. Diagnose context (GPS, permission, or Wi-Fi) with explicit action paths.

3. Applied HCI Laws (Hick's, Fitts's, Jakob's)

Hick's Law

*Decision time increases with options.* Porter lists 5+ uncategorized trucks.
Redesign: Group into "BHK Moving", "Small Loads", and pin "Recommended" presets.

Fitts's Law

*Target distance & size impact speed.* secondary edits use small targets near corners.
Redesign: Bring main CTA inputs directly within comfortable thumb sweep.

Jakob's Law

*Users project mental models from peer apps.* Users expect Uber/Ola-like clarity.
Redesign: Standardize booking step layout structure with clear sequence logic.

4. Expected User & Business Outcomes

Product Redesign Elements
  • Simplified Fleet Select: Visual hints and real-world utility tags on cards.
  • Inverted Info Hierarchy: Total cost elevated above billing/GST details.
  • Contextual Errors: Intelligent diagnosis for location errors.
Projected Impact on Metrics
  • Booking Abandonment: Lower
  • Task Completion Speed: Up to 3x Faster
  • Mismatched Vehicles: Reduced
  • Support Tickets (L1): Decreased
